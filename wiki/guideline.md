- Blue Bin (Containers)

            - Black plastic food containers

            - Bottles, jugs, tubs & lids (empty & rinsed)

        ‚ùå Not Accepted: Biodegradable/compostable plastics, motor oil bottles, hazardous waste containers.

    - Glass & Metal

        ‚úÖ Accepted:

            - Glass bottles & jars (clear or coloured)

        ‚ùå Not Accepted: Drinking glasses, ceramics, window glass, scrap metal (pots/pans).

    - Polycoat

        ‚úÖ Accepted:

            - Coffee cups (hot & cold beverage cups)

            - Ice cream tubs
            - Milk & juice cartons
            - Juice boxes (Tetra Paks)

        ‚ùå Not Accepted: Straws & stir sticks.

- Black Bin (Paper/Fibre)

    - Paper

        ‚úÖ Accepted:

            - Newspapers, flyers, magazines
            - Office paper, envelopes
            - Paper gift wrap (non-foil)
            - Paper bags

        ‚ùå Not Accepted: Waxed paper, soiled paper/tissues (use Green Bin), foil wrapping paper.

    - Cardboard

        ‚úÖ Accepted:

            - Corrugated cardboard (flattened)
            - Boxboard (cereal, cracker boxes)
            - Pizza boxes (clean parts only)

        ‚ùå Not Accepted: Soiled pizza boxes (greasy parts go in Green Bin).

- Green Bin (Organics)

    - Compost

        ‚úÖ Accepted:

            - Food scraps (meat, bones, dairy, veg)
            - Soiled paper & cardboard (pizza boxes)

        ‚ùå Not Accepted: Plastic bags (unless certified compostable, but generally discouraged), pet waste (in some programs).

    - Yard Trimmings

        ‚úÖ Accepted:

            - Leaves (deciduous and evergreen)
            - Grass clippings
            - Woody debris (small branches, twigs, hedge prunings)
            - Garden plants (dead flowers, weeds)
            - Seasonal items (natural Christmas trees, pumpkins, corn stalks)

        üìù Preparation & Containers:

            - Kraft paper bags (e.g., Bag To Earth).
            - Reusable fabric bags (e.g., heavy-duty lawn bags) or open bins.
            - Bundled branches: Twine only, <4ft (1.2m) long, <2in (5cm) diameter.
            - Weight limit: <50lbs (23kg) per bag/bundle.

        ‚ùå Not Accepted:
            
            - Plastic bags (even "biodegradable" ones are often prohibited).
            - Rocks, soil, sod.

        üí° Beneficial Reuse:
            - Mulching: Scatter leaves/clippings to retain moisture.
            - Grasscycling: Leave clippings on the lawn.
            - Home Composting: Mix greens (grass) and browns (leaves).

- Household Hazardous Waste

    - Batteries

        Types: AA, AAA, 9V, Button cells, Rechargeable batteries.
        Where to Take:
        Hardware Stores: Home Depot, Rona, Lowe's.
        Electronics Stores: Staples, Best Buy.
        Public Libraries: Look for the "Call2Recycle" battery tubes in the lobby.

        ‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Prep:
        Tape the terminals: Place clear tape over the ends of 9V and Lithium batteries to prevent fires.
        Store in a non-conductive container (plastic or cardboard) until drop-off.

    - Paint (Latex, Oil, Stains)

        Where to Take:
        Retailers: Most paint and hardware stores (Rona, Home Depot, Dulux) accept liquid paint return.
        Note: Search "Product Care Recycling" to confirm the store participates.

        ‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Prep:
        Lids on tight: Ensure the can is sealed and not leaking.
        Original Labels: Staff need to see the original label to identify the type.

    - Empty Cans:

        Dry/Empty Metal Cans: Remove lid and place in Blue Bin.
        Dry/Empty Plastic Cans: Place in Blue Bin.

    - Chemicals (Pesticides, Solvents, Bleach, Pool Chemicals)

        Where to Take:
        HHW Depots ONLY: These cannot be returned to retailers.
        You must attend a City of Ottawa Household Hazardous Waste Depot (Mobile events running April‚ÄìNovember).
        Permanent Depot: The Trail Road Waste Facility often accepts these (check hours).

        ‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Prep:
        Never mix chemicals: This can create deadly fumes.
        Leaking containers: Place the leaking item into a larger plastic tub or bucket and label the outside.

    - Motor Oil & Antifreeze

        Where to Take:
        Automotive Shops: Many Canadian Tire locations and "Jiffy Lube" style shops.

        ‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Prep:
        Do not mix with gasoline, paint, or water.
        Maximum container size is usually 20L.

    - Light Bulbs (CFLs & Fluorescent Tubes)

        Where to Take:
        Retailers: Rona, Home Depot, Lowe's.
        Note: Regular incandescent (old school) bulbs go in the Garbage.

        ‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Prep:
        Handle carefully to avoid breakage (CFLs contain mercury).
